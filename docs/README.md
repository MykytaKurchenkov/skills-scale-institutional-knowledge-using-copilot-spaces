# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

## Overview

OctoAcme uses an iterative, customer-focused project management approach with clear ownership (PM + Product Lead), lightweight initiation via a project one-pager, prioritized planning into shippable increments, regular execution rhythms (standups, demos), and a focus on continuous improvement through retrospectives and tracked action items. Core artifacts include the one-pager, backlog with acceptance criteria, risk register, and release/runbooks.

## Project Lifecycle

OctoAcme projects follow a structured lifecycle across seven key phases:

1. **Initiation** — Validate business need, align stakeholders, and confirm go/no-go
2. **Planning** — Break work into shippable increments, identify dependencies, and create release plan
3. **Execution & Tracking** — Deliver features with daily standups, demos, and quality gates
4. **Risk Management & Communication** — Monitor risks, escalate blockers, and keep stakeholders informed
5. **Release & Deployment** — Move features to production safely with rollback plans
6. **Retrospectives & Continuous Improvement** — Capture learnings and convert them into action items
7. **Roles & Personas** — Clear responsibilities for Developers, Product Managers, and Project Managers

## Key Artifacts

Across all projects, maintain these core artifacts:

- **Project Charter / One-pager** — Problem, goal, success metrics, timeline, stakeholders, risks
- **Roadmap & Release Plan** — Prioritized features, milestones, and release schedule
- **Sprint/Iteration Backlog** — Backlog items with acceptance criteria and estimates
- **Definition of Done (DoD)** — Clear standards for what "complete" means
- **Risk Register** — Identified risks with impact, likelihood, and mitigation plans
- **Retrospective Notes & Action Items** — Learnings and tracked improvements

## Communication Cadence

All projects follow this regular rhythm:

- **Daily standups** (15 min) — Progress, blockers, dependencies
- **Weekly PM + PdM sync** — Alignment on priorities and decisions
- **Twice-weekly team standup** — Delivery team coordination (or as agreed)
- **Monthly stakeholder updates** — Progress and milestone status
- **Sprint/Release retrospectives** — Capture learnings and action items
- **Ad-hoc escalations** — For urgent risks or blockers
